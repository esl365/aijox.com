## Video Resume Essentials

### Optimal Length
- **2-3 minutes ideal**
- Maximum 5 minutes
- Shorter is better - keep it concise

### Technical Requirements
- **Resolution:** 1080p (Full HD) minimum
- **Format:** MP4 (most compatible)
- **File size:** Under 100MB
- **Orientation:** Horizontal (landscape)
- **Audio:** Clear, minimal background noise

### Equipment Needed

**Minimum:**
- Smartphone (iPhone 8+ or Android equivalent)
- Good lighting (natural window light)
- Quiet location

**Recommended:**
- DSLR camera or modern smartphone
- Tripod or stable surface
- Ring light or softbox lighting
- Lapel microphone (clip-on)
- Backdrop (plain wall or bookshelf)

## Content Structure

### 1. Introduction (20-30 seconds)

**Include:**
- Your name
- Current location
- Years of teaching experience
- Teaching specialty/grade level

**Example:**
> "Hello! I'm Sarah Johnson, a certified elementary teacher with 8 years of experience teaching grades 3-5. I'm currently based in California and excited to share why I'm passionate about international education."

### 2. Educational Background (30-45 seconds)

**Highlight:**
- Degree and major
- Teaching certification
- Additional qualifications (TEFL, Master's, etc.)
- Relevant training

**Example:**
> "I hold a Bachelor's degree in Elementary Education from UCLA and a California teaching credential. I'm also TEFL certified with a 120-hour advanced certification, and I recently completed my Master's in Educational Technology."

### 3. Teaching Experience (45-60 seconds)

**Focus on:**
- Grade levels taught
- Subjects/specializations
- School types (public, private, international)
- Key achievements
- Technology integration

**Example:**
> "Over the past 8 years, I've taught in both public and charter schools, working with diverse student populations. I specialize in differentiated instruction and have successfully implemented project-based learning programs. Last year, my students showed a 25% improvement in reading comprehension scores."

### 4. Teaching Philosophy (30-45 seconds)

**Demonstrate:**
- Your approach to education
- Student-centered beliefs
- Classroom management style
- Cultural sensitivity

**Example:**
> "I believe every student can succeed with the right support and encouragement. My classroom is a safe space where students feel comfortable taking risks and learning from mistakes. I use a blend of traditional and innovative teaching methods, always adapting to meet individual student needs."

### 5. Why International Teaching (20-30 seconds)

**Express:**
- Motivation for teaching abroad
- Cultural awareness
- Adaptability
- Long-term goals

**Example:**
> "I've always been passionate about cross-cultural education and global citizenship. Teaching internationally allows me to broaden my perspective while making a positive impact on students from diverse backgrounds. I'm particularly excited about immersing myself in new cultures and bringing that experience back to my teaching."

### 6. Closing (15-20 seconds)

**End with:**
- Thank you message
- Availability
- Call to action
- Smile and enthusiasm!

**Example:**
> "Thank you for taking the time to watch my video. I'm available for immediate placement and excited about the opportunity to contribute to your school community. I look forward to speaking with you soon!"

## Video Production Tips

### Before Recording

1. **Write a script**
   - Don't memorize word-for-word
   - Use bullet points
   - Practice 3-5 times

2. **Choose your location**
   - Quiet room
   - Minimal distractions in background
   - Professional setting (home office, classroom, bookshelf)

3. **Test your setup**
   - Check lighting
   - Test audio quality
   - Verify camera angle

### Lighting Tips

**Natural Light:**
- Film facing a window
- Soft, diffused light is best
- Avoid direct sunlight (creates harsh shadows)

**Artificial Light:**
- Ring light positioned at eye level
- Two-point lighting for depth
- Avoid overhead lights (create shadows)

**Best time:** Morning or late afternoon for natural light

### Audio Quality

- **Test your microphone** before final recording
- **Close windows** to reduce outside noise
- **Turn off** AC, fans, appliances
- **Use a quiet room** with minimal echo
- **Consider:** Hanging blankets to reduce echo

### Camera Setup

**Position:**
- Camera at eye level
- Distance: Arm's length away
- Framing: Head and shoulders visible
- Leave space above head (headroom)

**Background:**
- Clean, uncluttered
- Professional (bookshelf, plain wall)
- Avoid: Unmade beds, messy rooms, distracting items

### During Recording

1. **Body language**
   - Sit up straight
   - Smile naturally
   - Make eye contact with camera
   - Use hand gestures (but keep them in frame)

2. **Speaking**
   - Speak clearly and at moderate pace
   - Vary your tone (avoid monotone)
   - Show enthusiasm
   - Pause between sections

3. **Energy level**
   - Higher energy than normal conversation
   - Think: TV presenter, not conversation
   - Stay animated throughout

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

### Content Mistakes
1. **Too long** - Over 5 minutes loses attention
2. **Reading from script** - Sounds robotic
3. **Too much information** - Focus on highlights
4. **Negative comments** - Never bad-mouth previous employers
5. **Irrelevant details** - Stay focused on teaching

### Technical Mistakes
1. **Poor lighting** - Dark, shadowy face
2. **Bad audio** - Echo, background noise
3. **Vertical video** - Use horizontal/landscape
4. **Shaky camera** - Use tripod
5. **Cluttered background** - Distracting items

### Presentation Mistakes
1. **Looking away from camera** - Breaks connection
2. **Monotone delivery** - Sounds disinterested
3. **Too casual** - Inappropriate clothing/setting
4. **No smile** - Seems unfriendly
5. **Rushed** - Speaks too fast

## What to Wear

### General Guidelines
- **Business casual** minimum
- **Professional** but not overly formal
- **Solid colors** (avoid busy patterns)
- **Avoid** white or black (can cause exposure issues)
- **Best colors:** Navy, grey, burgundy, forest green

### For Men
- Collared shirt or polo
- Optional: Blazer or cardigan
- Well-groomed appearance

### For Women
- Blouse or professional top
- Optional: Blazer or cardigan
- Minimal jewelry
- Natural makeup

## Editing Your Video

### Recommended Tools

**Free:**
- iMovie (Mac)
- Windows Video Editor (PC)
- DaVinci Resolve (Advanced, free version)
- CapCut (Mobile)

**Paid:**
- Adobe Premiere Pro (Professional)
- Final Cut Pro (Mac)
- Camtasia (Screen recording + editing)

### Basic Edits

1. **Trim** beginning and end
2. **Remove** long pauses or mistakes
3. **Add** simple title slide (name, position)
4. **Include** contact information at end
5. **Adjust** audio levels
6. **Export** in 1080p MP4

### Optional Enhancements
- Subtle background music (very low volume)
- Text overlays for key points
- B-roll footage (classroom scenes, if available)
- Smooth transitions between sections
