# UAE Teaching Visa Guide: How to Get a Work Visa for Dubai & Abu Dhabi

The United Arab Emirates offers some of the most lucrative teaching opportunities globally, with tax-free salaries, generous benefits, and a high quality of life. This guide covers everything you need to know about obtaining a work visa for teaching positions in Dubai, Abu Dhabi, and other emirates.

## Why Teach in the UAE?

- **Tax-free salaries:** Keep 100% of your earnings
- **High compensation:** AED 9,000-20,000/month ($2,500-5,500 USD)
- **Comprehensive benefits:** Housing, flights, health insurance
- **Modern infrastructure:** World-class schools and facilities
- **Central location:** Easy travel to Europe, Asia, Africa

## Visa Types

### Employment Visa (Work Visa)
- **Sponsored by employer** - School handles all paperwork
- **Duration:** 2-3 years (renewable)
- **Includes:** Residence permit for you and dependents

### Visit Visa
- **NOT for working** - Only for job interviews
- **Duration:** 30-90 days

## Basic Requirements

### Educational Qualifications
- **Bachelor's degree minimum** (accredited institution)
- **Teaching license** (required for most positions)
- **TEFL/TESOL** (120+ hours, if no teaching license)
- Degree must be attested by:
  - Home country's Ministry of Foreign Affairs
  - UAE Embassy in home country
  - UAE Ministry of Foreign Affairs (upon arrival)

### Experience Requirements
- **2+ years teaching experience** (for licensed teachers)
- **Fresh graduates:** May qualify for training positions
- **Subject specialists:** Higher demand for STEM, English, Math

### Age Requirements
- **21-60 years old** (general requirement)
- Some schools accept teachers over 60 with extensive experience

### Additional Requirements
- **Clean criminal record** - Attested background check
- **Good health** - Medical fitness certificate from UAE-approved clinic
- **Valid passport** - Minimum 6 months validity

## Required Documents

### For Visa Application
1. **Passport copy** (valid 6+ months)
2. **Passport-sized photos** (white background)
3. **Educational certificates** (attested)
4. **Teaching license** (if applicable, attested)
5. **Employment contract** from UAE school
6. **Degree attestation** (see below)

### Attestation Process

Documents must be attested in this order:

1. **Home country:**
   - Notary public
   - Ministry of Foreign Affairs
   - UAE Embassy/Consulate

2. **UAE upon arrival:**
   - Ministry of Foreign Affairs
   - Ministry of Education (for teaching credentials)

**Timeline:** 4-8 weeks
**Cost:** $200-500 USD (varies by country)

## Application Process

### Step 1: Secure Job Offer
Find a school willing to sponsor your visa. Reputable schools will handle most visa procedures.

### Step 2: Document Attestation
Begin attestation process immediately - this takes the longest.

### Step 3: Entry Permit
School obtains entry permit from UAE immigration:
- Processing: 2-4 weeks
- Valid for 60 days to enter UAE

### Step 4: Enter UAE
- Arrive within 60-day entry permit window
- School arranges airport pickup (usually)

### Step 5: Medical Fitness Test
- Blood tests (HIV, Hepatitis B/C, TB screening)
- Chest X-ray
- Conducted at government-approved clinic
- Results: 2-3 days
- Cost: AED 300-500 (~$80-135 USD)

### Step 6: Emirates ID
- Biometric registration
- Mandatory for all residents
- Card issued within 2 weeks
- Valid for duration of visa
- Cost: AED 370 (~$100 USD)

### Step 7: Residence Visa Stamping
- Visa stamped in passport
- Final step in process
- Cost: AED 2,000-3,000 (~$545-815 USD) - usually paid by employer

## Timeline

**Total Process:** 6-12 weeks (from job offer to visa stamping)

- Job search: 1-3 months
- Document attestation: 4-8 weeks
- Entry permit: 2-4 weeks
- Medical & Emirates ID: 1-2 weeks
- Visa stamping: 1 week

## Salary & Benefits

### Typical Package (International Schools)

**Salary:** AED 9,000-20,000/month ($2,500-5,500 USD)

**Benefits typically include:**
- Housing allowance or accommodation
- Annual flight tickets (home and back)
- Health insurance (comprehensive)
- End-of-service gratuity
- Tuition discount/free for children
- Professional development allowance

### Tax-Free Income

UAE has **zero income tax**, meaning your entire salary is take-home pay.

### Savings Potential

Teachers typically save 50-70% of salary:
- Monthly savings: $1,500-3,500 USD
- Annual savings: $18,000-42,000 USD

## Emirates-Specific Considerations

### Dubai
- Most competitive market
- Higher cost of living
- Diverse student population
- Extensive expat community

### Abu Dhabi
- Government-run ADEK schools (higher salaries)
- Slightly lower cost of living than Dubai
- Strong emphasis on curriculum standards

### Sharjah, Ajman, RAK
- More affordable living
- Growing demand for teachers
- Smaller expat communities
- Shorter commutes

## Common Pitfalls to Avoid

1. **Working on visit visa** - Illegal and can result in deportation
2. **Incomplete attestation** - Most common delay
3. **Medical fitness failure** - Certain conditions disqualify
4. **Contract issues** - Read employment contract carefully
5. **Unverified schools** - Research employer thoroughly

## Dependent Visas

You can sponsor:
- Spouse
- Children under 18 (or 24 if studying)
- Parents (if you meet salary requirements)

**Minimum salary for sponsorship:**
- Spouse: AED 4,000/month
- Spouse + children: AED 6,000-10,000/month
- Parents: AED 20,000/month

## Visa Renewal

- Renew 2-3 months before expiration
- Updated medical test required
- Usually processed within 2 weeks
- Employer handles renewal process

## FAQs

**Can I teach in UAE without a teaching license?**
Some private training centers accept TEFL certification, but international schools require a teaching license.

**How long does the visa process take?**
6-12 weeks from job offer to visa stamping.

**Can I switch employers?**
Yes, but new employer must sponsor new visa. There's a 30-day grace period after contract end.

**Do I need to know Arabic?**
Not required. Most schools use English as the primary language.

**What if I fail the medical test?**
Certain conditions (HIV, Hepatitis B, TB) result in visa denial and deportation.

**Can I bring my family?**
Yes, but you need minimum salary (see Dependent Visas section).
